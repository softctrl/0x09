How you can see, in the big-endian architecture the more significant bit becomes first, when in the little-endian architecture, the less significant bit becomes first.
Technically, there is not a huge difference between this two architectures. And sincerely i don't see many advantages in one strategy or in other. I believe that in a cert moment of our history, many manufacturers are using your own solution for the same problem, and when was necessary to they different architecture to communicate on network, has be implicit the need to translate this data.
Some architectures can manage this both architectures (little and big endian), they are called BI-ENDIAN.
The need to intermediate this architectures are evidenced in networks:
In a network we have both, little and big endian, and for convention the bits will be transported as big-endian architecture. This only can be possible if those bits are translated between this two possibilities.
On RFC 1700 we can see in "Data notation" when says:
"The convention in the documentation of Internet Protocols is to

express numbers in decimal and to picture data in "big-endian" order



[COHEN].  That is, fields are described left to right, with the most



significant octet on the left and the least significant octet on the
right."
So, at this moment is clear that those architectures who uses the endianness different from big-endian, that they will need a way do translate between endianess forms.
